# Car Wash App — Business Idea

## Overview

A booking platform that allows customers to pre-book car wash slots at listed car washes, eliminating queues and wait times. Long term, the app expands to offer vehicle pickup and drop-off so customers never have to leave their home or office to get their car washed.

---

## Problem Being Solved

- Car washes in South Africa — especially popular ones on weekends — have long queues with unpredictable wait times
- Customers either waste time sitting in a queue or avoid going altogether
- Car wash owners have no way to predict or manage demand — staff and resources are either overwhelmed or idle
- No dominant platform exists in SA that aggregates car washes the way Uber Eats aggregates restaurants

---

## Core Features (MVP)

### 1. Customer Side
- Browse nearby car washes on a map
- View available time slots for the day or week
- Book a slot in advance — arrive and go straight in, no queue
- Select wash type (e.g. Basic Exterior, Full Valet, Interior Clean) and see pricing upfront
- Receive confirmation and reminder notifications

### 2. Car Wash Owner Side
- Apply to be listed on the platform
- Set available slots, pricing, and service types
- View upcoming bookings and daily schedule
- Mark bookings as complete

### 3. Pickup & Drop-off (Phase 2)
- Customer books a wash and selects pickup
- A driver collects the car from their home or office
- Car is washed and returned within an agreed timeframe
- Customer tracks their car in real time
- Premium pricing for this service

---

## Business Model Options

**Option A: Commission Per Booking**
- App takes 10-15% of every booking made through the platform
- No upfront cost for car washes to list
- Aligns incentives — app only earns when car washes earn

**Option B: Monthly Listing Fee**
- Car washes pay a flat monthly fee (e.g. R299-599/month) to be listed
- Simpler to manage, predictable revenue
- Risk: car washes with low bookings may churn

**Recommended for MVP:** Start with Option A (commission). Easier to sign up car washes since there's no upfront risk for them. Switch to or add a hybrid model once volume is proven.

---

## Expansion Path

**Phase 1 — MVP:** Booking platform, one city (Cape Town or Joburg), 10-20 car washes listed

**Phase 2 — Pickup & Drop-off:** Add driver network for vehicle collection and return. Could use existing staff at car washes or recruit independent drivers.

**Phase 3 — Additional Services:** Expand beyond washing to include services offered at car washes such as tyre inflation, quick oil checks, windscreen chip repairs, and detailing packages.

**Phase 4 — Fleet & Corporate Accounts:** Offer monthly plans for businesses with vehicle fleets (delivery companies, car rental firms, ride-hail drivers). Recurring, high-volume revenue.

**Phase 5 — Multi-city Rollout:** Expand to all major SA cities then other African markets.

---

## Key Advantages

- **Low technical complexity** — a booking app is a solved problem. Build time is fast.
- **Immediate local value** — solve a real daily frustration for car owners
- **Low customer acquisition cost** — partner with car washes who market to their own existing customers first
- **Sticky for car wash owners** — once their scheduling runs through the app, switching costs are high

---

## Key Risks & Challenges

- **Supply side is the hard part** — getting car wash owners to sign up and trust a new platform. Many are small, informal operators who may resist technology adoption.
- **No-shows** — customers who book but don't arrive hurt car wash owners. Need a cancellation policy and possibly a small deposit system.
- **Fragmented market** — most car washes in SA are small independent operations, not chains. Sales effort to sign them up is high.
- **Margins on commission** — car washes operate on thin margins. Taking 15% may feel steep to owners. Pricing must be negotiated carefully.

---

## Go-To-Market Strategy

**Start with one premium car wash.** Rather than listing many car washes immediately, partner exclusively with one well-known, high-traffic car wash in your city. Build the product around their specific workflow. Prove it reduces their queue and increases their revenue. Use that as a case study to sign up the next 10.

**Weekend warriors are your core user.** Target people who wash their car every weekend. These are habitual, repeat customers — the most valuable users for a booking platform. Market on Instagram and through local community groups.

**Corporate fleet accounts early.** Even 2-3 small businesses with 5-10 vehicles each gives you predictable recurring revenue from day one. Approach local delivery companies, estate agencies, or plumbing/electrical businesses who run vehicle fleets.

---

## Questions to Explore

- Should the MVP be a web app (faster to build) or native mobile app (better UX for bookings)?
- How do we handle payments — upfront booking deposit or pay at the car wash?
- What does the car wash owner dashboard need to look like for a non-technical operator?
- How do we structure the pickup/drop-off driver network — employees or contractors?
- Which city should we launch in first and why?
- How do we handle cancellations and no-shows fairly for both sides?
- Is there an opportunity to white-label this platform for a specific car wash chain?
- What would make a car wash owner choose this over just using WhatsApp bookings?
